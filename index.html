Current Research and Projects

Lightning and Atmospheric Rivers

A recent case study identified an atmospheric river that coincided with an extreme number of lighting strikes and explored the synoptic and mesoscale features of this electrifying AR on the southern California coast. Future research will examine the relationship between other ARs and lightning activity.

Atmospheric Rivers in High Mountain Asia

Precipitation and glacial melt in High Mountain Asia provides water resources to hundreds of millions of people yet the precipitation regimes in this region are not fully understood. Current research identifies the climatology of Atmospheric Rivers that reach High Mountain Asia and contribute between 30-60% of total winter precipitation.

Pyclivac

Pyclivac is a series of python programs and tutorials for beginner climate scientists that was specifically developed for new python learners in the Climate Variations and Changes research group at UCSB. The main focus of this project is introducing Python 3 and map plotting capabilities.
